of meaning.

Hello! I'm Nya Feinstein, a PhD student at the University of Pittsburgh working in the PICSO Lab under Dr. Yu-Ru Lin. My research sits at the intersection of Computational Social Science and Computational Linguistics, examining how cultural and contextual nuances embedded in language shape international discourse and information exchange across networks.

This work is deeply intertwined with my commitment to Belarusian and Slavic Studies, where I combine computational linguistics, political analysis, education, and language advocacy.

Research Interests
  • How can deep learning help us understand nuanced meaning across multiple languages?
  • What do dynamic networks of communication reveal about how information spreads?
  • How can quantitative insights influence security, mitigate conflict, and lead to more productive approaches to international relations?
  • In what ways can AI be applied for social good, and what are the ethical issues we must consider?
  • How can digital humanities approaches deepen our understanding of Belarusian and Slavic studies?
